## What is LearnChain?

LearnChain is an open-source blockchain layer built on top of the Youth For Change TT platform. It automatically issues on-chain tokens to young people aged 14–25 when they complete verified learning milestones, creating a transparent, tamper-proof record of achievement and a self-sustaining incentive economy for youth development across the Caribbean.

---

## The Problem

Young people in Trinidad and Tobago and the wider Caribbean have no tangible incentive to complete online learning programmes and no verifiable, portable proof of informal achievement. Donors and development partners funding youth programmes have no transparent way to confirm that impact is actually being delivered.

---

## The Solution

LearnChain adds a blockchain layer to an existing, operational youth development platform. When a youth completes a verified milestone, a smart contract automatically issues Change Tokens (CHG) on the Polygon network. These tokens are publicly auditable, permanently recorded and redeemable for tangible rewards including partner gift cards, local business discounts and digital rewards.

---

## How It Works

1. Youth completes a verified learning milestone on the Youth For Change TT platform
2. Milestone verification contract confirms completion
3. ERC-20 smart contract automatically issues Change Tokens (CHG) to youth wallet
4. Achievement is permanently recorded on the Polygon blockchain
5. Donor or partner views real-time impact dashboard showing verified outcomes
6. Youth redeems tokens for tangible rewards through partner network

---

## Smart Contract Architecture

Two core contracts govern the system:

*1. CHG Token Contract (ERC-20)*
Governs the issuance, supply and management of Change Tokens on the Polygon network.

*2. Milestone Verification Contract*
Triggers automatic token issuance when a verified learning activity is completed on the platform. Connects the Youth For Change TT platform to the blockchain layer via a lightweight API bridge.

Both contracts are currently in pre-development. They will be written, tested on Polygon testnet and submitted for formal third-party security audit before any mainnet deployment.
